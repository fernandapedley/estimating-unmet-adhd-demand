This page summarises the main findings from the analysis of ADHD prevalence, referrals and waiting-list activity in England between December 2024 and December 2025.

---

## 1. ADHD prevalence increased substantially

![Prevalence and Waiting List Trends](images/chart_1_prevalence_waiting_list.png)

Recorded ADHD prevalence increased by approximately 54% during the reporting period. Waiting-list activity increased by a similar proportion, suggesting that growth in recorded ADHD prevalence was accompanied by increasing demand for assessment and treatment services.

---

## 2. Referral activity remained high

![Referral Trends](images/chart_2_referrals.png)

Open referrals increased steadily throughout the reporting period, while new referrals showed a more gradual increase. This suggests that services were continuing to receive new demand while simultaneously managing large numbers of people already within the referral pathway.

---

## 3. Adult age groups accounted for most growth

![Prevalence by Age Group](images/chart_3_prevalence_by_age.png)

Adults aged 25 and over represented the largest proportion of recorded ADHD prevalence throughout the reporting period. The largest absolute growth was also observed within the adult age groups.

---

## 4. Adult services experienced higher levels of service pressure

![Waiting List Ratio](images/chart_5_waiting_list_ratio.png)

Adult age groups consistently showed higher waiting-list demand relative to recorded prevalence than younger age groups. This pattern remained stable throughout the reporting period.

---

## 5. Potential unmet demand may be concentrated within adult pathways

![Potential Unmet Demand Indicator](images/chart_7_unmet_demand_indicator.png)

The potential unmet demand indicator remained consistently high throughout the reporting period. While this should not be interpreted as a direct measure of unmet need, it provides a useful indication of service pressure and highlights where further investigation may be warranted.
