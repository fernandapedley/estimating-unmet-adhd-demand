# Estimating Potential Unmet ADHD Assessment Demand in England

---

## Overview

Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) services across England are experiencing increasing demand, with many patients facing lengthy waits for assessment and treatment.

This capstone project uses publicly available NHS England data to explore ADHD prevalence, referral activity and waiting-list trends between December 2024 and December 2025.

The aim of the project is not to measure unmet need directly, as the available data does not allow this. Instead, it investigates whether patterns in prevalence, referrals and waiting-list activity can provide useful insight into where service pressure may be greatest and where potential unmet demand may exist.

The project demonstrates how data analytics techniques can be applied to a real-world healthcare challenge using publicly available healthcare datasets.

---

## Project Objectives

The project sought to:

* Estimate the expected level of ADHD assessment demand using published prevalence estimates.
* Analyse referral and waiting-list trends using NHS England data.
* Compare estimated need with observed service activity.
* Explore patterns that may indicate potential service pressure.
* Demonstrate the application of data analytics techniques within a healthcare setting.

---

## Key Questions

This analysis explores the following questions:

1. What level of ADHD assessment demand might be expected based on published prevalence estimates?
2. How have referrals and waiting-list indicators changed over time?
3. To what extent does observed service activity reflect estimated population need?
4. What do these findings suggest about potential unmet demand for ADHD assessment services in England?

---

## Headline Findings

The analysis identified several consistent patterns across the dataset:

* Recorded ADHD prevalence increased substantially between December 2024 and December 2025.
* Waiting-list activity increased alongside prevalence.
* Referral volumes remained high throughout the reporting period.
* Adult age groups accounted for most of the observed growth.
* Adults aged 18–24 and 25+ consistently showed higher waiting-list demand relative to recorded prevalence than younger age groups.
* The difference between adult and children's services remained remarkably stable throughout the year.

Taken together, these findings suggest that service pressure may be concentrated within adult ADHD pathways and provide a useful starting point for exploring potential unmet demand.
